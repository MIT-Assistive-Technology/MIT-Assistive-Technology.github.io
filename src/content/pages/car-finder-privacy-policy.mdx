<h1>
	<strong>Privacy Policy for CarFinder</strong>
</h1>
<p>
	<strong>Effective Date</strong>: 12/31/2025
	<br />
	The MIT Assistive Technology Club is dedicated to safeguarding the privacy and confidentiality of
	your personal information. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, share, and protect
	your personal data when you use our mobile app, CarFinder, and its services.
</p>
<p>
	CarFinder is an assistive technology application designed to help blind and visually impaired
	users locate their vehicles. The app uses camera data, on-device object detection, compass data,
	and depth information to provide navigational assistance.
</p>
<h3>
	<strong>Data We Collect</strong>
</h3>
<p>When you use our mobile app, we collect the following types of personal information:</p>
<ol>
	<li>
		<strong>Vehicle Information</strong>
		<br />
		We collect information that you provide about your vehicle, including make, model, color, and
		license plate number.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: When you input "Blue Honda Civic, license ABC123", we store this information
			to help you locate your specific vehicle.
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Camera Data</strong>
		<br />
		With your explicit consent, we access your device's camera to capture images of your
		surroundings to identify vehicles.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: When you scan your environment, the app captures images to detect and
			identify vehicles that may match your description.
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Sensor Data</strong>
		<br />
		We collect data from your device's compass and depth sensors to provide navigational assistance.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: We use compass data to give you directional guidance like "Your car is 20
			feet ahead to your right."
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Device Information</strong>
		<br />
		We gather technical data about your mobile device, such as device type, operating system, and
		app version.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: We collect your device type (iPhone, Android) to ensure the app is
			compatible and runs smoothly on your device.
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>IP Address</strong>
		<br />
		When using our services, your IP address may be collected and shared with our third-party
		service providers.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: Your IP address may be logged when making API calls to our vehicle
			recognition service.
		</p>
	</li>
</ol>
<h3>
	<strong>How We Use Your Information</strong>
</h3>
<p>We use the data we collect for the following specific purposes:</p>
<ol>
	<li>
		<strong>Vehicle Identification and Navigation</strong>
		<br />
		We use your vehicle information (make, model, color, license plate) and camera data to identify
		your vehicle and provide navigational guidance.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: When you scan your surroundings, we match captured images against your
			vehicle description to help you locate your car.
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Third-Party Processing</strong>
		<br />
		We share images captured by your camera with Eydea Recognition (an external company) for vehicle
		make, model, and color recognition.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: When you scan the parking lot, images are sent to Eydea Recognition to
			identify vehicles in the frame.
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>AI-Powered Matching</strong>
		<br />
		We share API results from Eydea Recognition along with your vehicle description with OpenAI to
		match detected vehicles with your specific vehicle.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: OpenAI helps determine if the "blue sedan" detected in an image matches your
			"Blue Honda Civic" description.
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Service Improvement</strong>
		<br />
		We analyze usage patterns and app performance to improve functionality and user experience.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: We may analyze how often the app successfully identifies vehicles to improve
			our detection algorithms.
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Technical Support</strong>
		<br />
		We use device information to troubleshoot issues and provide technical support.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: If you report a problem, we may use your device information to diagnose
			compatibility issues.
		</p>
	</li>
</ol>
<h3>
	<strong>Legal Basis for Processing</strong>
</h3>
<p>We process your data based on the following legal grounds:</p>
<ol>
	<li>
		<strong>Consent</strong>
		<br />
		We process your personal information when you have given us explicit consent, such as agreeing
		to receive marketing communications or enabling location tracking.
		<br />
		<em>Example</em>: You can withdraw your consent at any time by changing your app settings or
		unsubscribing from emails.
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Performance of a Contract</strong>
		<br />
		We process your data to fulfill the terms of service you agree to when using our app. CarFinder
		is provided free of charge, with no payment required.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: When you use CarFinder, we process your vehicle information and camera data
			to provide the vehicle identification service as described in our terms of service.
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Legitimate Interests</strong>
		<br />
		We may process your data for purposes aligned with our legitimate business interests, such as
		improving our app or conducting user analytics.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: Analyzing app usage data helps us improve the user experience and develop
			new features.
		</p>
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Compliance with Legal Obligations</strong>
		<br />
		We may process your data to comply with legal requirements or respond to legal requests when
		required.
		<p>
			<em>Example</em>: We may need to retain certain usage data for compliance with accessibility
			laws or respond to valid legal requests from authorities.
		</p>
	</li>
</ol>

<h3>
	<strong>Data Security</strong>
</h3>
<p>
	We implement a range of technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data from
	unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse.
</p>
<ol>
	<li>
		<strong>Encryption</strong>
		<p>
			We use encryption to protect data during transmission between your device and our servers.
		</p>
		<em>Example</em>: Images captured by your camera are encrypted during transmission to our
		vehicle recognition service.
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Image Processing</strong>
		<p>
			Images captured by your camera are processed for vehicle identification but are not
			permanently stored by us or our third-party providers.
		</p>
		<em>Example</em>: After processing your images to identify vehicles, the images are not retained
		in our systems.
	</li>
	<li>
		<strong>Access Controls</strong>
		<p>
			We limit access to your personal data to only those team members who need it to perform their
			job functions.
		</p>
		<em>Example</em>: Only developers working on improving vehicle recognition have access to
		anonymized usage data.
	</li>
</ol>

		comply with legal requirements.
